🚫 Section 7: Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even seasoned gamers fall into these traps. Save yourself hours of headaches — dodge these five common killers of performance and stability.

⚠️ 1. Ignoring Driver Updates

Problem: Outdated GPU or chipset drivers cause crashes, glitches, or lost performance.

Fix: Always download drivers straight from NVIDIA, AMD, or your motherboard maker — skip third-party “driver updater” software.

What’s the latest stable driver for my NVIDIA RTX 3060, and how do I clean install it properly?

🧼 2. Letting Bloatware Linger

Problem: Prebuilt PCs often come packed with junk apps that eat up RAM and slow your boot.

Fix: Use tools like Revo Uninstaller and Autoruns to clean them out completely — not just “uninstall.”

What are the safest programs to uninstall on a new Windows 11 prebuilt gaming PC?

🌡️ 3. Ignoring Temps & Airflow

Problem: Overheating causes thermal throttling, crashes, and shortens your hardware’s life.

Fix: Monitor with HWiNFO, dust your fans, replace thermal paste every 1–2 years, and manage airflow.

How do I check my CPU and GPU temperatures safely while gaming?

📦 4. Blindly Maxing Out Settings

Problem: Ultra presets can trash performance for barely-noticeable gains.

Fix: Start on High or Medium. Then selectively boost textures and shadows — and ditch motion blur.

Which graphics settings can I safely lower in Cyberpunk 2077 without making it look bad?

🧬 5. Skipping BIOS/UEFI Updates

Problem: You could be missing vital performance patches or hardware compatibility fixes.

Fix: Check your motherboard’s support page. If your BIOS is more than a year old, consider updating (carefully!).

How do I safely check and update the BIOS on my ASUS B550 motherboard?

🔥 Wrap-Up: It’s not just about FPS — it’s about stability, longevity, and smart habits. Avoiding these slip-ups will keep your rig purring and your games butter-smooth.
