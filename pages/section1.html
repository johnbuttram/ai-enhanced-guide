Section 1: Getting Started

Know Thy Rig: Prepping ChatGPT with Your PC Specs

If you want ChatGPT to help you optimize your gaming PC, you’ve gotta speak its language — and that starts with knowing what’s under the hood.

You don’t need to memorize hardware jargon or recite your GPU’s VRAM like a mantra. You just need a basic snapshot of your setup. Once ChatGPT has that info, it can give you targeted, relevant advice — not just generic tips.

Why This Matters

  • Some fixes only apply to AMD or NVIDIA cards — ChatGPT needs to know what you're rocking.
  • Older CPUs may bottleneck your GPU — ChatGPT can help spot that.
  • RAM, SSD, and even your monitor refresh rate play a role in game performance.

What You'll Learn in This Section

  • How to quickly get a summary of your PC specs
  • How to feed that info to ChatGPT in a way it understands
  • How to save this as a reusable prompt for future help

Pro Tip: Save your system summary as a prompt snippet so you can re-use it anytime.

You are an expert PC optimization wizard. I’ll give you my system specs, and I want you to analyze them for performance bottlenecks and give me specific, actionable advice to improve gaming performance. My System Specs: - CPU: [Insert CPU Name] - GPU: [Insert GPU Name] - RAM: [Insert Size & Speed] - Storage: [Insert SSD/HDD info] - OS: [Insert Windows Version] - Display: [Resolution & Refresh Rate] What are 3 things I can do to boost performance?

New to ChatGPT? Here’s a Crash Course

You don’t need to be a prompt wizard to get great results — but you do need to treat ChatGPT like a teammate, not a search engine. Here’s how to get started:

  • Be specific: Instead of “fix my game,” say “My GPU is an RTX 3060 and I’m getting FPS drops in Elden Ring. What could be causing it?”
  • Give context: Share your specs, OS version, and even what you’ve already tried.
  • Use follow-ups: ChatGPT remembers context in the same conversation, so build on it. Ask “What’s next?” or “How do I do that step?”
  • Use bullet-style prompts: ChatGPT handles lists well. Try: “Here’s my system info. Give me 3 performance tips tailored to this.”

Pro Tip: Save your favorite prompts in a Notepad file or text doc so you can reuse them later.
