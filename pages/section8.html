Prompt Packs & Templates: Your AI Arsenal
Section 8: Prompt Packs & Templates

If you’ve followed this guide, you’ve already written better prompts than 90% of users. Now it’s time to build your own AI toolkit — reusable, remixable prompts that you can fire off whenever your rig starts acting up.

What’s a Prompt Pack?

A prompt pack is a bundle of focused, pre-written prompts you can use for a specific scenario. Think of it like a cheat sheet for ChatGPT. You don’t rewrite them from scratch — you just tweak the inputs.

Prompt Pack Example: FPS Drops

Describe the scenario: “I’m playing [game] and FPS drops occur [frequency].” Add setup: “Specs: RTX 3070, Ryzen 5 5600X, 16GB RAM.” Ask for help: “What should I look at first?” Follow-up: “Can you walk me through how to test CPU vs GPU bottlenecks?”

Prompt Pack Example: Clean Up & Speed Up

“Give me a step-by-step system cleanup guide for Windows 11 focused on gaming performance.” “Include things like temp files, startup apps, unnecessary services, and bloatware.”

Prompt Template: Modular Style

[Describe the Problem] e.g. “FPS drops when entering new zones in [Game Name]” [System Specs] e.g. “RTX 3060, Ryzen 5600X, 16GB RAM, 1440p, SSD” [Ask] e.g. “What should I troubleshoot first?”

Where to Store Prompt Packs

  • Text files in a “Prompt Vault” folder
  • Markdown in Notion or Obsidian
  • GitHub Gist (for syncing + sharing)
  • ChatGPT’s custom instructions (save in memory)
Pro Tip: Don’t hoard prompts — refine and re-test them. The best ones evolve over time just like your setup.
